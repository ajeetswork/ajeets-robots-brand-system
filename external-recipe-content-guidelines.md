# External Recipe Content Guidelines — Ajeets Robots

These rules govern how Ajeets Robots content may reference, link to, or draw inspiration from third-party recipes (e.g., King Arthur Baking and similar food/recipe publishers). The goal is to use recipe-adjacent occasions as creative context for our Printify products — never to republish, rebrand, or imply endorsement from the recipe source.

---

## 1. Link to Recipe Sources

- Always include a direct, public URL to the original recipe page.
- Use the canonical publisher URL (e.g., `https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/recipes/...`), not an aggregator, scraper, or repost.
- Place the link near the first mention of the recipe name so readers can find the source immediately.
- Do not use affiliate links, URL shorteners, or redirect wrappers unless the recipe publisher explicitly provides them.
- If content appears in a format that supports clickable links (blog posts, emails, social bios), make the recipe name itself the link. In plain-text formats, put the full URL on its own line directly below the recipe name.
- Keep a record of every external recipe referenced in a given piece of content: recipe title, publisher name, and URL. This can be a simple list in a content tracker or at the bottom of the draft.
- If a recipe URL goes dead, update or remove the reference — do not leave broken source links in published content.

## 2. Write Original Summaries

- Describe the recipe or the occasion it suits in your own words. Never copy the publisher's intro paragraph, headnote, or step text.
- Summaries should be short (1–3 sentences) and focus on the *activity or occasion* the recipe represents — e.g., "a quick summer bake for casual entertaining," "a small-batch weeknight treat," "a two-person date-night project."
- Do not reproduce ingredient lists, measurements, yields, temperatures, or step-by-step instructions. If readers want to bake it, they follow your link to the source.
- Summaries must add context that is specific to Ajeets Robots content — explain why the occasion is relevant to our audience or to a product we're featuring. A generic rephrasing of the publisher's own description is not sufficient.
- Run a quick comparison check before publishing: paste your summary next to the source page's description and confirm there is no sentence-level overlap.

## 3. Avoid Copied Wording or Images

- Do not copy any text from the recipe page — including the recipe title's stylized formatting, taglines, pull quotes, headnotes, tips, or user comments.
- Recipe names themselves (e.g., "Strawberry Shortcake Cookies") may be used verbatim for identification, with a link to the source. This is the only verbatim text permitted.
- Do not download, screenshot, crop, or repost any images from the recipe publisher's site, social accounts, or CDN. This includes product shots, process photos, and lifestyle images.
- If visual content is needed, create original photography/illustration featuring Ajeets Robots products — or use stock imagery with a valid commercial license that is unrelated to the recipe source.
- Do not mimic the publisher's layout, color scheme, typography, or visual framing in a way that could confuse readers about the source of the content.
- If quoting is unavoidable (e.g., for a brief attribution), keep it to a short phrase, use quotation marks, name the publisher, and link to the source. Full-sentence quotes from recipe pages are not allowed.

## 4. Avoid Affiliation Claims

- Never state or imply that Ajeets Robots is partnered with, sponsored by, endorsed by, or affiliated with any recipe publisher.
- Do not use phrases like "in collaboration with," "together with," "presents," "official," or "approved by" in connection with an external recipe source.
- Do not use the recipe publisher's logo, brand marks, or trade dress in Ajeets Robots content.
- Include a simple disclaimer when recipe-inspired content is published, especially in email/newsletter campaigns and blog posts. Example: *"Recipe references are for inspiration only. Ajeets Robots is not affiliated with [Publisher Name]. To bake this recipe, visit [link]."*
- If the recipe publisher contacts us asking to remove a reference, comply promptly — even if the use was within fair-use guidelines.
- Do not register, claim, or bid on the recipe publisher's brand terms in advertising, search, or social platforms in connection with Ajeets Robots content.

## 5. Describing How Printify Products Relate to the Activity

- Every Ajeets Robots product mentioned in recipe-inspired content must have a clear, non-baking role that connects to the *occasion or activity* — not to the recipe itself.
- Good framing: the product fits into the scene around the activity. Example: a tumbler for iced drinks at a summer get-together where cookies also happen to be served; a cozy sweatshirt for a quiet weeknight; a mug for a wind-down routine; a pillowcase for making a living room feel warm for a night in.
- Bad framing: presenting the product as a baking tool, ingredient, or part of the recipe ("our apron for baking these cookies," "use our measuring cup," "the official [Recipe Name] tee"). Ajeets Robots does not sell kitchen tools or food products — keep the product role separate from the cooking/baking process.
- For each product featured, include: Product Title, Price, a one-sentence non-baking role (how it fits the occasion), and the public Printify product link.
- Do not claim the product was designed for, tested with, or recommended by the recipe publisher or recipe author.
- If a product does not have a natural, believable role in the activity's setting, do not force it in. Skip that product and pick one that fits — or skip the recipe tie-in for that piece of content entirely.
- Product descriptions in recipe-inspired content should use the same plain-language, accessible structure defined in the Ajeets Robots Accessible Product Guide: What it is → Features → Specs → Care Instructions, with care info clearly separated.
